Some of the rioters start pushing physically to get to the stage. I thank my good intuition for keeping my mother home: this riot will excalate quickly. Lisa is charging the riot, leading on the front. For a second there, I admire her guts and courage, and I feel inspired to follow her.

«Please, children of Aether, don't use your anger against the Mother of all mothers!»

The priest tries to dissuade us, but the more he speaks, the more I feel the anger rising and taking control. Because when he mentions the mother of all mothers, the only one I think about is mine, and I don't accept her being belittled under the name of something that's actually harming us all. I move with the rioters, gaining my space among the chairs and all the submitians sitting on them. Many of them get up and start running away, sensing the upcoming dangers; others try to stop us, especially the older men, but can't.
