The stairs are, like always, endless and crowded. Lunch time means traffic on the elevators because of all the workers going home for a break: we are left with this only option. The more we walk down, the more the hallways become dirty, noisy, messy and dark: many people in this area believe in Mother Gloom and are convinced that darkness will save them all. So they close the only windows to the world, leaving us with artifical lights (which often flicker), dust and rotten air.

Step by step I go down. People bump into me, not caring at all about personal space. Here, that's the matter: in Submitia personal space is non-existent, our fathers sold it to have at least roofs on their heads.
