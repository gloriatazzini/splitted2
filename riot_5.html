My rage feeds off of his words. I think of my mother, already severely damaged by the fog of Submitia: she struggles to remember her past and to imagine a different future. And he robs her, and all the others, of the opportunity of understanding and making sense of the present, blurring a mind that's already burnt.

«We are, Submitians, the engine, the power, the great children of Mother Gloom!»

I can't take this anymore, and my mates around me seem to think the same thing. We start yelling from the back, trying to stop this nonsense.

«There's no mother Gloom! There's a fog and it's probably intoxicating all of us»

«We are invisible to you!»

«We don't want you mother Gloom, we want our city to see us!»

«Clear the fog!»
