Conrad Hudson's office is the messiest place I've ever entered. And I live in a 30 squared meters apartment. Here, in the tiny room they've given to him, he keeps an unbelievable amount of papers and books, alongside tools and parts of robotic equipments. He clears a tiny space on his desk and turns on the lamp to illuminate the device. After trying different combination of clicking and tapping on the buttons, he extracts a screwdriver from his drawer and looks at me with a strange light in his eyes.

«Okay, seems like we have to open this up to see if it actually still works.»

He takes out the small screws and opens the little box on the back of the device. In front of us is an intricate labirynth of wires and mechanisms. We look at each other.

«This is going to take a while» Hudson tells me, as he grips a magnifying glass and starts examining the inside of the device.
