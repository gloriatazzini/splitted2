On my way back home, I sense a feeling of oppression on my chest. It becomes harder to breathe at every step I take on the stairs. My head spins a little, so I am forced to stop on the landing and lean on the walls; I close my eyes and take some deep breaths to calm down. I can't understand my reaction, even though his words rest in my mind as I try to give a sense to them.

It's impossible he didn't remember me. Something was done to him, and I try to think about the many different possibilities: it could have been the tear gas. Or maybe he was taken by one of those men on the way home, even though none of them seemed to have followed us. There's an entire world of information I have to shine a light on.

My only option is talk to Hudson about it.
